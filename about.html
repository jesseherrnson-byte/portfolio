Jesse was fascinated by computers from a very young age. At age six, he created a simple "make it to the end" game that did not at all resemble Snakes and Ladders. It was Jesse's first experience experimenting with a format and trying to “find the fun.”

Jesse continued pursuing his interest in computer science at URJ 6 Points Sci-Tech Academy, a science and technology camp. There, Jesse first learned how to code (Processing/Java) and the basics of how to create games. He took the Video Game Design workshop five times as a camper and returned for many years as a staff and leadership member.

During his time at Wesleyan University, Jesse took an intensive course on the game design process taught by Professor Christopher Weaver, founder of Bethesda Softworks. This course challenged teams of coders, designers, and artists, to create an educational children's video game, which would be playtested at a local elementary school. His team's game, One Earth, won the competition. In following semesters, Jesse became a course assistant to support future students in the creation of their games and to iteratively refine the course curriculum. Jesse graduated from Wesleyan in May of 2024, with a double major in Computer Science and Music, a minor in Film, and a focus on game design. In his final semesters, he supported the development of the Game Design minor at Wesleyan.

Currently, Jesse is a software engineer at PwC in Boston, MA, where he works on internal AI projects for the streamlining of processes in the firm.
