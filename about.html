Jesse was born on November 1, 2002, in Miami, Florida. He grew up on Long Island, New York, and was fascinated by computers from a very young age. His first consoles, like many others his age, were the Nintendo DS and Wii, with which he played games like Pokemon and The Legend of Zelda, widening what he thought was possible with computers. From the start, Jesse wanted to actually make games; he made his very first board game when he was 6 years old, a simple "make it to the end" type game that did not at all resemble Snakes and Ladders, which he drew with marker on some poster paper that he then laminated. It was a 3-player game, as he noticed that almost all board games went down to two, and he wanted to change the format.

Jesse owes a lot of his continued interest in computer science to the camps he went to as a child, most prominently URJ 6 Points Sci-Tech Academy, a Jewish science and technology camp in Massachusetts. It was there that Jesse first learned how to code (Processing/Java) and some of the basics of game design. He would end up taking the Video Game Design workshop five times in his few years there, and would return back many years later as a staff and leadership member.

Jesse's interest in computer science continued well into high school and into his time at Wesleyan University in Middletown, CT. There, he took an intensive course on the game design process taught by Professor Christopher Weaver, founder of Bethesda Softworks. This course put students into multi-disciplinary teams of coders, designers, and artists, with the challenge of creating an educational children's video game over the course of three months. This game was playtested at a local elementary school and would eventually become One Earth, the winning game of the semester as determined by a panel of professionals. In following semesters, Jesse became a course assistant for the course, and would reinforce his passion for education while working with Professor Weaver. Jesse graduated from Wesleyan in May of 2024, with a double major in Computer Science and Music, a minor in Film, and a focus on game design. He was present for the first few talks of developing a Game Design minor at Wesleyan; unfortunately, he would graduate before it was formalized.

Currently, Jesse is a software engineer at PwC in Boston, MA, where he works on internal AI projects for the streamlining of processes in the firm.
