INK is a game that I originally created in school, but soon became my largest personal project. The original pitch was to create a game that taught children about the concept of failure –– that it wasn't something to be scared of, but an opportunity for further experimentation and exploration. This developed into a fairly simple concept: an inky black blob in a white room with white walls. The blob can be launched, and whenever it hits a wall, it leaves a splatter and dissovles, respawning at the beginning of the level. Every time the player "dies," they have the opportunity to try again, with just a little more information than they had last time.

Since the creation of the original idea, I have designed roughly 100 levels for INK, all utilizing different concepts and the four different power-ups which change the behavior (and color) of the inky character blob. I designed these each on small sticky notes, meticulously noting what was in each level, and then reorganizing them in order to ensure a good difficulty curve. I worked in collaboration with art programmer Junior Siguencia, who ensured the game had a cohesive visual style, with a sleek, simplistic feel.

In addition to the main programming and physics systems for the game, I also worked on the music and sound design. I created an adaptive sound system which altered the background music based on which power-ups the player had collected throughout the course of a level. While composing the music and designing the sound effects, I aimed for a very simple, lo-fi sound to complement the game's visual style.
